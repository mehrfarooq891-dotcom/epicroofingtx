Why We Know Houston Roofing

Texas climates are completely brutal on materials. Generic, non-local builders don't design for our severe atmospheric humidity, coastal winds, and high-impact microbursts.

☄️

Extreme Hail Seasons

Between March and June, Southeast Texas experiences large, concentrated hail that fractures shingle maps. This breaks down the protective granular layer, exposing the underlying fiberglass layer. We utilize heavy-impact rated Class 4 impact shingles to resist high-velocity vertical impacts.

🌀

Hurricane-Grade Wind Prep

Direct atmospheric pressure from Gulf Hurricanes and strong tropical gales can exceed 110-130 MPH. We run specific multi-tier high-velocity starter shingles and reinforced GAF heavy-grip fasteners. This ensures shingles lock together and resist upward suction from microbursts.

☀️

Oppressive Heat & Humidity

Long, humid Houston summers cause massive heat build-up inside attics, exceeding 140°F. If not ventilated properly, this literally bakes asphalt shingles from underneath, causing rapid blistering, cracking, and decay. We install specific ridge vents to provide continuous natural ventilation.
