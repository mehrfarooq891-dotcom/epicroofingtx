Houston Storms Cause More Roof Damage Than You Can See

After hail or high winds, the visible damage is only part of the picture. Hidden damage to underlayment and decking can cause leaks and mold months later. Here's what we look for:

HAIL BRUISING

Large hail knocks granules loose from your shingles. Even when not visible from the ground, this damage shortens your roof's lifespan and gives water a path in.

WIND UPLIFT DAMAGE

High winds get under shingle edges and peel back underlayment. The damage isn't always obvious — but one rain storm later, you'll have a leak.

MOISTURE AND ROT

Water past damaged shingles sits in your decking and attic framing. Left unrepaired, it causes rot, mold, and structural damage that costs far more to fix.

Frequently Asked Questions — Houston Roofing

How much does a new roof cost in Houston?

On average, a new shingle roof in Houston costs between $6,500 and $15,000 depending on the size of your home, the pitch of your roof, and the specific shingle materials you choose. Premium materials like metal roofing or Class 4 impact-rated shingles will cost more but offer superior durability. Contact us for a completely free, detailed estimate tailored to your home.

Will my insurance cover roof damage?

Yes, if your roof has suffered sudden damage from storm events like high winds, hail, or a fallen tree limb, your homeowner's insurance policy will typically cover the cost of repair or replacement. You will only be responsible for paying your policy deductible. Epic Roofing & Construction LLC is highly experienced in documenting storm damage and working with insurance adjusters to make sure your claim is filed correctly.

How long does a roof replacement take?

Most standard residential roof replacements in the Houston area can be completed in just 1 to 2 days! Our experienced crews work efficiently to tear off the old shingles, repair any damaged decking, install new underlayment, and lay down the new roof. We also perform a meticulous cleanup using magnetic sweeps to make sure zero nails or debris are left behind in your yard.

Do I need a permit to replace my roof in Houston?

Yes, the City of Houston and most surrounding municipalities (like Katy, Sugar Land, and Pearland) require a building permit for a full roof replacement. At Epic Roofing & Construction LLC, we handle the entire permitting process for you, ensuring that your new roof meets all local building codes and successfully passes any required post-installation inspections.

How much does roof repair cost in Houston TX?

For minor issues like a pipe boot leak, minor flashing damage, or a few missing shingles, roof repairs in Houston typically cost between $300 and $1,500. If the damage was caused by a recent storm, it may be fully covered under your homeowners insurance. We provide a free, honest inspection to let you know whether a repair is sufficient.

How do I know if I need roof repair or full replacement?

If your roof is under 15 years old with minor damage, repair is usually sufficient. Over 15 years with storm damage covering 30%+ of the surface, replacement is more cost-effective — and often fully covered by insurance. Our free inspection will tell you exactly what you need.

Houston Roof Replacement FAQs (2026 Guide)

How much does a roof replacement cost in Houston in 2026?

In Houston, a full asphalt shingle roof replacement typically costs $9,500–$16,000 for an average 2,000 sq ft home, depending on material grade and roof pitch. Metal roofing runs $18,000–$28,000. Storm damage claims can offset most of this if approved.

How long does a roof last in Houston's climate?

Asphalt shingle roofs in Houston typically last 15–20 years due to intense UV exposure and humidity, shorter than the national average of 20–25 years. Metal roofs last 40–50 years and better resist Houston's hail and wind events.

Does homeowners insurance cover roof replacement in Texas?

Texas homeowners insurance typically covers roof replacement if damage is caused by a covered peril like hail or wind — common in Houston. It does not cover damage from normal wear, age, or lack of maintenance.

How do I know if my roof has hail damage?

Signs of hail damage include dented or bruised shingles, granule loss visible in gutters, cracked shingle mats, and dented metal flashing or vents. A professional inspection within 12 months of a hailstorm is recommended for insurance claims.

What's the best time of year to replace a roof in Houston?

Fall (October–November) and early spring are ideal for roof replacement in Houston, avoiding peak hurricane season (June–November) and extreme summer heat, which can affect shingle installation and adhesive curing.
