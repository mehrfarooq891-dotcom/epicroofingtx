Does Your Roof Qualify For A Restorative Membrane?

Fluid membranes deliver massive savings, but only when installed on structurally stable substrates. Here is when coating works vs complete replacement:

✔️ When Coating makes sense

Your roof qualifies for a restoritve membrane if the current underlying insulation and decking show less than 20% localized moisture capture. If flashings are structurally stable but the surface membrane has dried out and shows hairline weathering splits, coating seals it flawlessly.

  • 🔸 Metal panels have surface rust but are structurally solid
  • 🔸 TPO sheets are intact but chalky with hairline cracks
  • 🔸 Modified bitumen is dry but maintains solid framing structure

🛑 When Replace is Mandatory

If deep infrared core probes verify that more than 25% of your roof insulation envelope is waterlogged, coating cannot help. Sealing over saturated insulation captures destructive water rot underneath, which can cause structural structural failures.

  • 🔹 Wide-scale structural wood rust or rot on decking beams
  • 🔹 Extreme composite insulation saturation (waterlogged core)
  • 🔹 Current roofing fails load code density limits

Silicone roof coatings are highly premium formulations engineered to resist continuous ponding rainwater on flat structures without breaking down. Elastomeric (typically acrylic-based) coatings are cost-effective water-based options that work beautifully on sloped metal roofs, providing flexible elongation but can slowly degrade if submerged in standing water indefinitely.

Once that duration expires, the roof can simply be deep-cleaned and re-coated, pushing a full replacement down the road indefinitely.
