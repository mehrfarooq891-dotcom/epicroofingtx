What Your Insurance Adjuster Is Looking For

Understanding adjuster criteria will help ensure your claim succeeds. Here is the science behind insurance storm approvals in Texas.

📏

1. The "Test Square" Impact Density

Adjusters outline a standard 10ft x 10ft area (a "square") on various roof slopes. They require a specific density of verified hail impacts (typically 8 to 15 strikes depending on slope and manufacturer specs) to authorize a full slope replacement.

👁️

2. Natural Wear vs. Acute Storm Bruising

Adjusters verify if damage is a result of hail bruising or simple weathering. "Blistering" from poor attic ventilation leaves circular pits but is flatly denied. Natural hail bruises leave soft, fractured underlining impacts that our digital scopes map out.

3. Creased Shingles & Wind Shear

Wind damage isn't just blown-off shingles. Adjusters look for horizontal creasing along the top of shingles, caused when high wind lifts the material and folds it upward. This creases the structural seal, destroying its waterproof barrier.

Common Houston Insurance Claim Mistakes

Failing to navigate the claims process correctly can cost homeowners thousands of dollars out of pocket. Avoid these common industry mistakes:

❌ 1. Delaying the Claim Filing Date

Waiting too long holds back approvals. Shingles degrade quickly under heat, and adjusters may blame wear rather than storm damage. Texas policies typically require claims to be filed within 1 year.

❌ 2. Signing Over Full Contingency Agreements Early

Be cautious of storm chasers demanding sign-overs before inspecting. Epic Roofing matches transparent scopes without aggressive tie-ins, keeping you fully in control of your policy.

❌ 3. Attempting to "Pocket" or Split Deductibles

Texas HB 2102 strictly prevents contractors from waiving deductibles. Homeowners are legally required to pay their specified deductible. Be cautious of contractors claiming otherwise.

❌ 4. Relying Heavily on "Out-of-State" Storm Teams

Storm-chasing crews move quickly between states. Choose a local, established firm that stands behind their workmanship and offers reliable multi-year physical warranties.

Supplementing Your Insurance Claim — What It Means

When an adjuster reviews a roof from the ground, they can only see the outer shingles. Once our crews start construction and tear off the old shingles, we often find hidden structural issues:

  • Water-soaked plywood structural decking
  • Rotted structural rafters that require structural repair
  • Venting transitions that do not meet current city building codes
  • Damaged flashing inside metal valley areas

This is where claims supplementing comes in. We photograph these hidden issues, submit structural proof to your adjuster via Xactimate, and negotiate the cost coverage. This ensures your roof is rebuilt correctly without out-of-pocket stress.

🚨 Important Compliance Notice: Texas law (HB 2102)

As of September 1, 2019, Texas state law strictly prohibits roofing contractors from waiving, absorbing, or discounting insurance deductibles. The homeowner is legally responsible for their specified policy deductible.

Contractors offering "free roof replacements" or promising to "split your deductible" are committing legal fraud under Texas state law, potentially exposing the homeowner to class-A misdemeanor charges. At Epic Roofing TX, we operate with 100% legal compliance and transparency.
