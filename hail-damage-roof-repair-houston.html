How Hail Damages Your Houston Roof

Let's explore the science of how freezing ice blocks falling at 80+ MPH destroy roofing membranes, leading to long-term framing rot.

🔬

Asphalt Bruising

Hail impact compresses the pliable asphalt layer down. This creates deep "bruises," fracturing the structural weave of the inner fiberglass backing mat.

☄️

Granule Displacement

Stones knock away the ceramic granules that protect shingles from the sun. Once exposed, the hot Houston UV radiation quickly degrades the dry sub-asphalt layer.

💧

Micro-Fracture Leaks

Fractured backing mats expand during freezing and summer expansion cycles. Within 12-18 months, water seeps through, rotting decking boards and attic insulation.

Houston Hail Season — April to June

Homeowners in southeast Texas deal with a specific meteorological window. From **early April to late June**, hot, humid Gulf moisture sweeps northward, colliding with cold atmospheric jet-streams from the Great Plains.

This collision creates severe supercell storms capable of spinning out high-velocity hail. Because shingles are often baking under warm spring temperatures, hot asphalt is highly susceptible to deep bruising from falling ice stones. Visible vs. Hidden Hail Damage

Many homeowners assume their roof is fine simply because they can't see fragments from the driveway. Here are the key differences:

👁️ Visible Storm Damage (Dents & Tears)

  • Dented Ridges & soft metals: Soft aluminum vents, gutters, box covers, and chimney linings display deep impact craters.
  • Torn, missing Shingles: Substantial hail impact combined with wind shear breaks off entire shingle fragments.
  • Shattered plastic boots: Cracked exhaust covers allow water to enter bathroom drywall ducts directly.

🛡️ Hidden Forensic Damage (Micro-Fractures)

  • Mat Fracturing: The shingle surface material appears stable, but the fiberglass backing mat underneath is split.
  • Latent Delamination: Seal sealant strips separate weeks after storms due to wind shear lifting a weakened shingle.
  • Microspore Entry Paths: Hairline asphalt ruptures absorb dew moisture, prompting rapid rot on wood decking boards.
