Types of Wind Damage to Houston Roofs

High velocity air movement doesn't strike roofs equally. It creates specific structural stress zones that fail during severe gulf storms.

1. Shingle Blow-Off & Missing Tabs

When high gusts catch aged, dry, or poorly nailed shingles, they rip the entire piece cleanly off the deck, exposing the underlying roofing paper and raw OSB sheathing sheets directly to the downpour.

2. Edge Peeling & Perimeter Uplift

The edges and corners of your roof structure bear the strongest focal uplift pressures. If starter course pathways or drip edges lack structural asphalt cement seals, the wind peels massive shingle sheets backward.

3. Shingle Creasing & Hidden Lift

Wind gusts lift shingle tabs up, breaking their thermal sealant bond and folding them backward. This leaves visible horizontal crease lines, snapping the internal fiberglass fabric and rendering them permanently useless.

4. Falling Debris Structural Puncture

Severe wind shear snaps heavy oak and pine branches, sending heavy debris crashing down. These impacts crack roof rafters, tear shingle barriers, and create major, active rainwater entry tunnels.
