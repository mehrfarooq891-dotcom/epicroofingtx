Why Houston Roofs Take More Storm Punishment Than Most US Cities

Evaluating wind and water hazards reveals that Houston sits directly in a highly active sub-tropical corridor. Our homes withstand intense atmospheric forces year after year, stemming from a combination of volatile coastal moisture and severe seasonal heat cycles. This high humidity, paired with summer heat exceeding 94°F, degrades asphalt granules prematurely, making shingles brittle and highly susceptible to storm damage.

In addition to humid conditions, the region experiences some of the highest hail frequencies in Texas, particularly during the peak storm season from April through June. Past historic weather events, including the devastation of Hurricane Harvey in 2017, damaged tens of thousands of rooftops overnight.
