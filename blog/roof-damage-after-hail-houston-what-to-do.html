Hail Hit Your Houston Home? Here's Exactly What to Do in the Next 48 Hours

A practical, stress-free guide written by local roofers to help you navigate insurance policies, stop sudden leaks, avoid storm-chaser scams, and get your home back to 100%.

📅 July 8, 2026 ⏱️ 11 Min Read 🏡 Local Guides

What should I do after hail damages my roof in Houston?

1. Stay safe on the ground; do not climb on the roof yourself.
2. Document visual yard damage and hailstone sizes with date-stamped photos.
3. Call your homeowner's insurance within 24 hours to report the loss.
4. Contact a trusted local roofer for a detailed professional inspection before the adjuster arrives.
5. Secure emergency tarping if you have active roof leaks.

It is a sound every Houston homeowner dreads. One minute you are sitting in your living room, and the next, a sudden thunderstorm unleashes what sounds like gravel pouring onto your roof. A severe hail storm houston roof event is incredibly stressful. Once the storm passes and the rumbling winds die down, you are left looking out the window wondering if your shingles survived, how bad the hidden damage might be, and what you need to do next to protect your property and family.

In the wake of severe weather, the choices you make in the first 48 hours can mean the difference between a smooth, fully covered insurance claim and thousands of dollars in unexpected out-of-pocket expenses. This guide walks you through the step-by-step hail damage steps houston homeowners need to follow to secure their homes, manage their insurance carriers, and ensure their roof after hail houston tx gets the professional care it deserves.

Step 1: Stay Safe — Don't Go on the Roof Yourself

When severe weather passes, your first instinct might be to grab a ladder and climb up to inspect the shingles. Please do not do this. Staying safe must be your absolute highest priority.

After a major storm, shingles can be physically compromised. Houston's intense humidity, combined with rain and loose, dislodged granules, makes the surface of wet shingles incredibly slick. Furthermore, storm winds can loosen structural decking or break shingle tabs, creating hidden slide hazards that you cannot spot from the top of a ladder. Falls from a two-story home in Katy, Cypress, or Spring can be catastrophic.

Professional roofers use specialized safety gear, including high-traction footwear, heavy-duty fall-arrest harnesses, and ridge hooks, to safely navigate steep roof pitches. Leave the high-altitude physical inspections to the professionals who are fully licensed, insured, and trained to walk damaged surfaces safely. You can gather plenty of crucial evidence without ever lifting your feet off the grass.

Step 2: Document Everything (Phone Photos, Video, Timestamp)

Evidence is the lifeblood of a successful storm damage insurance claim. While you should stay safely on the ground, your smartphone is an incredibly powerful tool for documenting immediate, date-stamped proof of the storm event.

Before the wind blows yard debris away or neighbors clean up, take a walk around your property and photograph the following items:

  • Hailstones: Pick up a few representative hailstones from your lawn or patio and photograph them next to an object of known size, like a quarter, a ruler, or a tape measure. This establishes clear proof of the physical size of the ice that struck your property.
  • Collateral Yard Damage: Insurance adjusters look for "collateral damage" around the property to confirm a storm's intensity. Take close-up photos of shredded leaves and broken branches on the lawn, heavily dented metal mailboxes, chipped paint on fences, and bruised window screens.
  • Exterior Fixtures: Inspect your metal gutters and downspouts. Hail often leaves distinct circular dents in aluminum gutters. Also, look at your exterior air conditioning condenser units; hail can easily bend or crush the delicate aluminum cooling fins.
  • Downspout Granules: Look at the ground where your gutter downspouts discharge. If you see deep piles of dark, sand-like asphalt granules, it is a primary indicator that your shingles have suffered severe impact damage, stripping away their protective mineral coating.

Step 3: Call Your Insurance within 24 Hours

Once you have visual evidence of hail on your property, you need to contact your insurance carrier or local agent to initiate the claim process. Waiting is one of the most common mistakes Houston homeowners make, and it can seriously jeopardize your coverage.

In Texas, insurance claims are highly dependent on specific dates of loss. When you call your carrier, they will match your address against historic meteorological radar logs to confirm that severe weather occurred on that exact date. If you wait several weeks or months to file, it becomes much easier for an insurance company to argue that your roof's deterioration was caused by normal wear and tear or separate, undocumented storms, giving them legal grounds to deny or reduce your claim payout.

Filing a claim within the first 24 to 48 hours establishes a clear, clean timeline, making it incredibly difficult for carriers to dispute the origin of the damage. Simply call your policy's claims hotline, provide the date of the storm, and share your initial photo documentation to get the ball rolling.

Step 4: Call a LOCAL Roofer for a Free Inspection Before the Adjuster Comes

Before an insurance adjuster ever steps foot on your lawn, you should contact a trusted, GAF-certified local roofing contractor to perform a detailed physical inspection.

It is important to understand that insurance adjusters are hired by and work directly for your insurance carrier. Their primary goal is to evaluate the loss from the insurance company's financial perspective. They are often incredibly busy—especially after a major storm hits the Houston area—and may only spend 15 or 20 minutes quickly looking over your roof. In their rush, they can easily miss subtle fracture lines, hail bruises on back slopes, or damaged flashing that could fail months down the road.

When you hire an experienced local contractor to perform a free roof inspection Houston assessment, you get a highly detailed, professional report outlining the full scope of the physical damage. A trusted local roofer will document every single compromised slope with high-resolution photos.

Best of all, your local roofing advocate will arrange to be on-site when the insurance adjuster arrives. By walking the roof slopes together, your roofer can point out hidden impacts, represent your interests objectively, and ensure the adjuster’s paperwork includes every piece of damage. This direct collaboration is the single most effective way to secure a fair, comprehensive approval for a full roof restoration.

Step 5: Get Temporary Tarping if There's Active Leaking

If the hail storm cracked shingles and caused an active ceiling leak inside your home, you must act immediately to stop the water. Under standard Texas homeowner insurance policies, you have a strict legal obligation to "mitigate further damage" to your property.

This means that if you have an active leak and do nothing to stop it, the insurance company may legally deny coverage for the subsequent damage to your interior drywall, insulation, flooring, and personal belongings. They will argue that those additional losses could have been easily avoided with prompt action.

Do not wait for the adjuster to visit before addressing an active leak. Call a professional roofer who offers immediate emergency tarping. Tarping isolates the damaged slopes and shields your home from incoming rain, keeping your interior dry and protected. The cost of emergency tarping is almost always fully covered by your insurance policy as a necessary mitigation expense, so keep the receipts.

What NOT to Do in the Next 48 Hours

Knowing what not to do after a storm is just as important as knowing what to do after hail damage houston events occur. Avoid these three critical mistakes:

  1. Do Not Attempt a DIY Patch: Trying to patch damaged shingles yourself with store-bought roofing cement or cheap tarps can void your existing manufacturer warranties. It can also make it look like you are covering up the damage, making it much harder for an insurance adjuster to evaluate the true scope of the storm loss.
  2. Never Sign an Assignment of Benefits (AOB): Some out-of-state "storm chasers" will ask you to sign an Assignment of Benefits (AOB) form before they even look at your roof. This document legally transfers all your rights, claims, and insurance payouts directly to the contractor. Keep control of your policy and your money; work only with contractors who provide clear, written estimates and let you handle payments.
  3. Do Not Accept Verbal Estimates or Illegal Deductible Schemes: Be highly suspicious of door-to-door sales representatives who offer to "absorb," "waive," or "pay" your insurance deductible. In the State of Texas, House Bill 2102 makes it a Class B misdemeanor for a contractor to pay, waive, or assist in hiding a policy deductible. Honest, reputable Houston contractors will never ask you to participate in an illegal insurance scheme. Always demand clear, detailed written estimates that break down all material and labor costs.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long do I have to file a hail damage claim in Texas?

In Texas, most insurance policies require you to file a storm damage claim within one year from the date of the storm event. However, some policies have shorter or longer windows, ranging from 180 days to two years. Regardless of your policy's specific terms, filing as quickly as possible is always the safest approach to prevent disputes over whether the damage was pre-existing or due to wear and tear.

Will my insurance go up if I file a roof claim in Texas?

Texas insurance laws prohibit insurance companies from raising your individual premium rates based solely on an act of nature, such as a hail storm or hurricane. However, if a major storm strikes your community and a high volume of claims are filed across your entire geographic area, the insurance company may adjust overall rate averages for your entire zip code, meaning everyone's premiums could rise regardless of whether they personally filed a claim.

Do I need a police report for hail damage?

No, a police report is not required for hail damage or storm-related claims. Insurance carriers rely entirely on meteorological weather reports, radar logs, on-site physical inspections, and photo documentation to verify that a storm occurred and caused damage to your property.

Can I get a roof replaced for free after hail in Houston?

No roof replacement is truly "free." By law, you are always responsible for paying your policy's designated wind/hail deductible out-of-pocket. Once your deductible is paid to your contractor, your insurance carrier is responsible for covering the remaining balance of the full replacement cost, ensuring your roof is restored to brand-new condition with minimal financial impact on your household.
