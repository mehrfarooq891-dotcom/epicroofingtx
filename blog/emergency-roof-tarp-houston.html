1. What is Emergency Roof Tarping?

Emergency tarping is the process of applying a heavy-duty, UV-resistant synthetic membrane over damaged portions of a roof to halt storm leaks temporarily. This is not a standard plastic tarp secured with loose bricks or weak bungee cords.

Professional tarping uses thick, woven woven-polyethylene tarps specifically designed to resist intense wind-shear. It is fastened securely to your roof deck using thin wood furring strips (anchor boards) nailed down at precise intervals. This airtight structural bond ensures the synthetic barrier cannot blow away during subsequent rainstorms. It typically remains strong and water-tight for 90 days, giving you ample time to coordinate with estimators, engineering analysts, and adjusters.

2. When Do You Need Emergency Tarping?

You should coordinate a professional emergency roof tarping service if your building exhibits any of the following critical conditions after a storm:

First, if tree branches or airborne debris have created physical structural holes in the decking. Second, if severe wind has peeled away large sections of shingles, exposing the raw, fragile underlayment or plywood substrate underneath. Third, when a major roof leak emergency houston is actively staining interior drywall or dripping into crawlspaces. Fourth, if massive hail hit your roof, fracturing the asphalt mats and immediately threatening the building's moisture resistance.

5. Is Emergency Tarping Covered by Your Insurance?

Yes. In almost all standard Texas property insurance policies, emergency mitigation measures are fully covered under the policy’s "Reasonable Repairs" or "Loss Mitigation" covenants.

As a homeowner, you have a formal legal duty under your insurance contract to take reasonable protective steps to prevent secondary damage (such as mold rot or drywall collapse) once a leak occurs. If you fail to have a professional install a protective tarp and key interior sections stain further, your insurance company can legally deny coverage for those secondary internal repairs.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long can an emergency roof tarp stay on?

A high-quality, professional synthetic tarp secured with proper anchor boards can safely remain on a roof for up to 90 days. However, you should aim to complete permanent structural repairs as quickly as possible to protect building materials from extreme coastal heat.

Should I try to tarp my own roof after a storm?

No. Climbing onto a storm-damaged, wet, or wind-swept roof without certified OSHA ropes or safety rigs is highly dangerous. Additionally, improper DIY nailing can cause severe secondary leaks and inadvertently hide critical wind-uplift signs needed for your insurance coverage review.

Does a tarp prevent mold from growing inside my attic?

A tarp stops active external rainwater from entering, which is the primary driver of attic mold. However, if water has already thoroughly soaked your attic insulation before the tarp was pinned, you must dry the space immediately to prevent spores from breeding under humid Houston heat.
